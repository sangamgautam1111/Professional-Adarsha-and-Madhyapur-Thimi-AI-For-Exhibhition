SECTION 3: HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
================================================================================

3.1 ANCIENT HISTORY
-------------------
Historical Period: Madhyapur Thimi has a history spanning over 2,000 years
Ancient Name: The area was known as "Madhyapur" meaning "Middle City"
Etymology of Thimi: 
- "Thimi" is derived from the Newari word "Thimi" meaning "capable people"
- Some sources suggest it comes from "Thimi" meaning "people who are capable"
- Local legend says the name reflects the hardworking nature of residents

Kingdom Era: Part of the ancient Kathmandu Valley civilization
Malla Period: Flourished as an important settlement during Malla dynasty
Lichchhavi Period: Evidence of settlements from Lichchhavi era (400-750 AD)

3.2 MEDIEVAL HISTORY
--------------------
Malla Dynasty Rule: Thimi was part of Bhaktapur Kingdom during Malla rule
Three Kingdoms Period: Aligned with Bhaktapur during the three Malla kingdoms era
Cultural Development: Significant development of Newari culture and traditions
Temple Construction: Many temples built during Malla period
Trade Route: Important point on trade routes within the valley

3.3 MODERN HISTORY
------------------
Unification Period: Incorporated into unified Nepal after 1768
Shah Dynasty: Continued development under Shah rulers
Municipality Formation: Established as a municipality
Merger: Traditional Thimi merged with surrounding areas
2015 Constitution: Became part of Bagmati Province under federal structure
2017 Restructuring: Maintained municipality status in local government restructuring

3.4 EARTHQUAKE IMPACT
---------------------
2015 Nepal Earthquake: Suffered significant damage
Magnitude: 7.8 on Richter scale
Damage: Many historical structures damaged
Reconstruction: Ongoing rebuilding efforts
Heritage Restoration: International support for heritage site restoration

SECTION 6: MAJOR FESTIVALS AND CELEBRATIONS
================================================================================

6.1 BISKET JATRA (BISKET FESTIVAL)
----------------------------------
Timing: Nepali New Year (Mid-April, around Baisakh 1)
Duration: 9 days
Significance: New Year celebration, Victory over serpent demons
Main Deity: Bhairav
Activities:
- Chariot pulling (Rath Jatra)
- Tongue-piercing ritual (at Bode)
- Lingo (pole) erection
- Sindoor Jatra (powder festival)
- Traditional sports and games
Location: Throughout Thimi, especially Ward 3 (Bode)

6.2 SINDOOR JATRA (VERMILLION FESTIVAL)
---------------------------------------
Timing: New Year's Day (Baisakh 1, mid-April)
Location: Thimi, especially Balkumari temple area
Activities:
- Throwing of sindoor (vermillion powder)
- Palanquin processions (Khat Jatra)
- Worship of Balkumari
- Music and celebration
- Carrying of Khats (palanquins) with deities
Significance: One of Nepal's most colorful festivals
Similarity: Often compared to Holi for its colors
Uniqueness: Unique to Thimi, not celebrated elsewhere in this form

6.3 BODE JATRA (TONGUE-PIERCING FESTIVAL)
-----------------------------------------
Location: Bode (Ward 3 of Madhyapur Thimi)
Timing: During Bisket Jatra (New Year)
Main Event: Tongue-piercing ritual
Participant: Selected individual from specific family
Ritual: Iron spike pierced through tongue
Significance: Sacrifice and devotion to deities
Belief: Protects community from evil spirits

6.4 OTHER MAJOR FESTIVALS
-------------------------
Dashain (Vijaya Dashami):
- 15-day festival
- Worship of Goddess Durga
- Family reunions

Tihar (Deepawali):

SECTION 16: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQ)
================================================================================

Q1: What is Madhyapur Thimi famous for?
A1: Madhyapur Thimi is famous for:
- Sindoor Jatra (vermillion powder festival)
- Traditional pottery making
- Bisket Jatra and Bode Jatra festivals
- Vegetable farming
- Rich Newari cultural heritage
- Traditional mask making

Q2: Where is Madhyapur Thimi located?
A2: Madhyapur Thimi is located in Bhaktapur District, Bagmati Province, Nepal. 
It is approximately 8 km east of Kathmandu and 4 km west of Bhaktapur Durbar Square.

Q3: How many wards are in Madhyapur Thimi Municipality?
A3: Madhyapur Thimi Municipality has 9 wards.

Q4: What is Sindoor Jatra?
A4: Sindoor Jatra is a unique festival celebrated on Nepali New Year's Day in 
Thimi where participants throw sindoor (vermillion powder) on each other while 
carrying palanquins of deities. It is one of the most colorful festivals in Nepal.

Q5: What is the tongue-piercing festival?
A5: The tongue-piercing festival is part of Bode Jatra, held during Bisket Jatra 
in Bode village (Ward 3). A selected individual from a specific family has an 
iron spike pierced through their tongue as a sacrifice to protect the community.

Q6: What crafts is Thimi known for?
A6: Thimi is particularly known for:
- Traditional pottery (clay pots, decorative items)
- Papier-mâché mask making
- Vegetable farming (though not a craft, it's a notable occupation)

Q7: What is the best time to visit Madhyapur Thimi?
A7: The best time to visit is during Nepali New Year (mid-April) to witness 
Sindoor Jatra and Bisket Jatra. October-November and February-April are also 
good times due to pleasant weather and various festivals.

Q8: What language is spoken in Madhyapur Thimi?
A8: Nepali is the official language, but Nepal Bhasa (Newari) is widely spoken 
among the local Newar community. English is also understood in tourist areas.

Q9: How do I get to Madhyapur Thimi?
A9: Madhyapur Thimi can be reached by:
- Bus from Kathmandu (Ratnapark or Kalanki)
- Taxi from anywhere in Kathmandu Valley
- Private vehicle via Ring Road or through Bhaktapur
- It is very close to Tribhuvan International Airport

Q10: What is the population of Madhyapur Thimi?
A10: The estimated population is approximately 80,000-90,000 people.
